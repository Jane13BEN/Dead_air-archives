Spontaneous Human Combustion (SHC)

Spontaneous Human Combustion (SHC) refers to the phenomenon where a person is reported to have caught fire and burned to death without any obvious external ignition source. This rare and mysterious event has puzzled researchers and the public for centuries. While there have been many reported cases of SHC, the phenomenon remains scientifically unexplained.

Theories Behind SHC

  • The Wick Effect: A theory that the body’s fat acts as a wick to fuel the fire once ignited.
  • Psychological or biological factors: Stress or rare medical conditions could contribute.
  • Environmental factors: Accidental ignition from alcohol or cigarettes.

The Case of Elisa Lam

In January 2013, 21-year-old Elisa Lam was found dead in a water tank at the Cecil Hotel in Los Angeles. Before her death, eerie elevator footage of her acting strangely became viral, leading to numerous theories about her mysterious fate.

Theories Behind Elisa's Death

  • Accidental Death: Lam may have experienced a psychotic episode, leading to her death.
  • Mental Illness: Elisa’s struggle with bipolar disorder might have contributed to her behavior and death.
  • Foul Play: Though there was no evidence, some speculate that someone was involved in her death.
  • Supernatural Theories: Due to the eerie footage and the hotel’s dark history, some claim paranormal forces were involved.
